# InkFoot Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want InkFoot to be a welcoming, respectful, and productive open source
community. Everyone who participates in the project is expected to help make
the community a place where people can collaborate without intimidation,
harassment, or hostility.

This pledge applies regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, level
of experience, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion,
gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, socioeconomic status, or any
other personal characteristic.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that helps build a healthy community include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respecting different viewpoints and experiences.
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback with care.
- Staying focused on what is best for the project and its users.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.
- Accepting responsibility, apologizing when appropriate, and learning from
  mistakes.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of behavior that is not acceptable include:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption of discussion.
- Insults, personal attacks, trolling, or deliberately inflammatory comments.
- Public or private harassment, including repeated unwanted contact.
- Publishing private information, such as physical or email addresses, without
  explicit permission.
- Unwelcome sexual attention, sexualized language, or sexual imagery in project
  spaces.
- Demeaning comments related to personal characteristics, identity, background,
  or level of experience.
- Any conduct that maintainers reasonably determine is inappropriate in a
  professional open source setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all InkFoot project spaces. That includes
repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, chat channels,
events, social media interactions made on behalf of the project, and any other
space where someone is representing InkFoot or its community.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the
project maintainers at:

**conduct@inkfoot.dev**

Reports should include as much relevant detail as you are comfortable sharing,
such as what happened, when it happened, where it happened, who was involved,
and any supporting links or screenshots.

Maintainers will review reports promptly and respectfully. Reports will be
handled with discretion, and information will only be shared with people who
need it in order to respond appropriately.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and
taking fair, consistent action when this Code of Conduct is violated.

Possible enforcement actions include:

1. **Correction**

   A private or public clarification explaining why the behavior was
   inappropriate, along with a request to stop or change the behavior.

2. **Warning**

   A formal warning that continued violations may result in stronger action.

3. **Temporary restriction**

   A temporary loss of access to project spaces, such as issue comments,
   discussions, pull requests, chats, or events.

4. **Permanent restriction**

   A permanent ban from some or all project spaces.

Maintainers may also remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code,
documentation, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that do not align
with this Code of Conduct.

Enforcement decisions should consider the impact of the behavior, the safety of
the community, the needs of the person who reported the issue, and whether the
person responsible has shown accountability.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers should:

- Apply this Code of Conduct consistently and in good faith.
- Avoid conflicts of interest when handling reports.
- Protect the privacy and safety of people who report incidents.
- Communicate enforcement decisions clearly when appropriate.
- Make reasonable efforts to prevent retaliation against anyone who reports a
  concern or participates in an investigation.
